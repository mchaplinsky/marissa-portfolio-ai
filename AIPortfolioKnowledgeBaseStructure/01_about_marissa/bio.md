# Marissa Chaplinsky — Bio

Marissa Chaplinsky is a Senior Product and UX Designer who specializes in designing complex digital products, AI-powered tools, and large-scale user experiences. Her work sits at the intersection of product strategy, user experience design, and intelligent systems, where she focuses on translating complex problems into intuitive, human-centered solutions.

Over the course of her career, Marissa has designed products across healthcare, government, enterprise software, startups, and education technology. She has led design initiatives for large-scale platforms including case management systems, internal enterprise portals, AI-powered product features, and consumer-facing digital experiences used by millions of users.

Most recently, Marissa has focused on designing AI-driven tools and intelligent systems that support human creativity and decision making. At TreeRing, she led the design of an AI-powered layout generation system that helped automate yearbook page creation while preserving human control and creative flexibility. The system reduced manual page creation time by **40–60 percent** while improving usability and adoption among non-technical users such as teachers, volunteers, and school administrators.

Her approach to design blends systems thinking with strong visual craft. Marissa frequently works at both the strategic and execution levels, helping organizations identify opportunities through research and analytics while also designing production-ready interfaces, interaction models, and scalable design systems.

In addition to her design work, Marissa often takes on leadership responsibilities within product teams. She leads design strategy, facilitates cross-functional workshops, aligns stakeholders around product decisions, and collaborates closely with engineering and product leadership to translate complex business problems into clear design solutions. Her work frequently involves guiding teams through ambiguous problem spaces and helping organizations make thoughtful decisions about product direction, system architecture, and user experience strategy.

Marissa is particularly interested in the responsible integration of artificial intelligence into products. Her work often includes defining AI behavior frameworks, designing guardrails and fallback states, and ensuring transparency and control for users interacting with automated systems. She believes AI should enhance human capabilities rather than replace them, and that thoughtful product design is critical to building trust in intelligent systems.

Throughout her projects, Marissa collaborates closely with product managers, engineers, leadership teams, and business stakeholders to align design decisions with business goals and technical feasibility. She frequently facilitates workshops, defines product strategy, and produces detailed design artifacts including service blueprints, journey maps, interaction flows, and high-fidelity prototypes.

Her design philosophy centers around **clarity, trust, and meaningful user outcomes**. She believes great products emerge when strong research, thoughtful systems design, and high-quality visual execution work together to solve real problems for both users and businesses.

Marissa is currently focused on designing AI-integrated product experiences, complex platforms, and scalable systems that support both enterprise and consumer users.
