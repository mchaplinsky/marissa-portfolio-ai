# Marissa Chaplinsky — Design Philosophy

Marissa Chaplinsky believes that great design sits at the intersection of human understanding, systems thinking, and visual clarity. Her work focuses on translating complex problems into intuitive, trustworthy product experiences that support both user needs and business goals.

Throughout her career, Marissa has worked on platforms that involve complex workflows, multiple stakeholders, and high-impact user decisions. Because of this, she approaches design not only as interface creation, but as a process of designing systems, behaviors, and decision frameworks that guide users through complex environments.

At the core of her design philosophy are three foundational principles: clarity, trust, and control.

**Clarity** ensures that users understand what a system is doing and how to interact with it. Clear information architecture, thoughtful interaction design, and strong visual hierarchy help reduce cognitive load and allow users to confidently navigate complex workflows.

**Trust** is built when systems behave predictably and transparently. Users should understand why something is happening within a product, especially when automation or artificial intelligence is involved. Designing for trust means clearly communicating system behavior, surfacing relevant information, and avoiding hidden automation that removes user agency.

**Control** ensures that users remain empowered when interacting with digital systems. Even when automation is present, users should always have the ability to adjust, override, or refine the system’s output. Marissa believes that the best intelligent products support human decision making rather than replacing it.

This philosophy has become particularly important in Marissa’s work designing AI-powered tools. When designing intelligent systems, she focuses on defining behavioral guardrails, fallback states, and clear feedback loops so users understand how the system is operating and can maintain confidence in its output.

Rather than designing AI as a black box, Marissa approaches AI systems as collaborative tools where humans and automation work together. Her work often involves defining system behaviors, interaction patterns, and product constraints that ensure intelligent features remain transparent, explainable, and useful.

In addition to systems thinking, Marissa values strong visual craft and clear interface design. She believes that thoughtful visual design plays a critical role in usability, helping guide attention, communicate hierarchy, and create products that feel intuitive and approachable.

Her design process typically blends research, strategic thinking, and iterative design exploration. She frequently begins by understanding the broader system environment including business goals, user needs, technical constraints, and organizational priorities. From there she develops frameworks, prototypes, and design artifacts that help teams move from ambiguity toward clear product direction.

Marissa also believes that great design happens through collaboration. She works closely with product managers, engineers, researchers, and leadership teams to align product vision, validate ideas, and ensure solutions are both technically feasible and user-centered.

Ultimately, Marissa approaches design as a way to help people solve problems, make decisions, and accomplish meaningful goals. Whether designing complex enterprise platforms, public service tools, or AI-powered systems, her goal is to create experiences that are intuitive, transparent, and empowering for the people who rely on them.
