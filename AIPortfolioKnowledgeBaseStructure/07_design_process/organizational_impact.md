# Organizational Impact

Design has the potential to influence not only product experiences but also how organizations operate and deliver value to users.

Marissa Chaplinsky approaches design as a strategic function that can improve both user outcomes and organizational efficiency.

By understanding how products interact with internal workflows, operational processes, and business goals, Marissa helps teams identify opportunities where design improvements can create broader organizational impact.

---

## Improving Operational Efficiency

Many product challenges are closely connected to operational workflows within organizations.

By analyzing how systems support internal teams, Marissa often identifies opportunities to simplify processes, reduce manual effort, and improve transparency within operational systems.

These improvements can lead to:

• more efficient internal workflows  
• reduced operational overhead  
• improved service delivery for end users  

Design solutions that address operational challenges often create value for both organizations and their users.

---

## Aligning Teams Around User Needs

Design artifacts such as journey maps, service blueprints, workflows, and prototypes help organizations develop a shared understanding of user experiences.

Marissa frequently uses these tools to help teams visualize how users interact with systems and identify opportunities for improvement.

This shared understanding helps organizations align around user-centered goals and make more informed product decisions.

---

## Creating Long-Term Product Improvements

Design decisions can influence how products evolve over time.

By focusing on scalable solutions, thoughtful system architecture, and adaptable interaction patterns, Marissa helps organizations build products that remain resilient as requirements change.

These long-term improvements support sustainable product growth and stronger user experiences.
