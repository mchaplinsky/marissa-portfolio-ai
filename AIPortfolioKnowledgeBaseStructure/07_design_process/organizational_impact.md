\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Organizational Impact\
\
Design has the potential to influence not only product experiences but also how organizations operate and deliver value to users. Marissa Chaplinsky approaches design as a strategic function that can improve both user outcomes and organizational efficiency.\
\
By understanding how products interact with internal workflows and business processes, Marissa helps teams identify opportunities where design improvements can create broader impact.\

## Improving Operational Efficiency\
\
Many product challenges are closely connected to operational workflows within organizations.\
\
By analyzing how systems support internal teams, Marissa often identifies opportunities to simplify processes, reduce manual effort, and improve transparency within operational systems.\
\
These improvements can lead to more efficient service delivery and better experiences for both internal teams and end users.\

## Aligning Teams Around User Needs\
\
Design artifacts such as journey maps, service blueprints, and prototypes help organizations develop a shared understanding of user experiences.\
\
Marissa frequently uses these tools to help teams visualize how users interact with their systems and identify opportunities for improvement.\
\
This shared understanding helps organizations align around user-centered goals.\

## Creating Long-Term Product Improvements\

Design decisions can influence how products evolve over time. By focusing on scalable solutions and thoughtful system architecture, Marissa helps organizations build products that remain adaptable as requirements change.\

These long-term improvements contribute to sustainable product growth and stronger user experiences.\
