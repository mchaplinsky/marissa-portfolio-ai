# Research to Design Workflow

Marissa Chaplinsky integrates research directly into the design process so that product decisions are grounded in real user insights rather than assumptions.

Rather than treating research as a separate phase, Marissa embeds research insights throughout the design lifecycle to ensure that solutions address real user needs and product challenges.

---

## Understanding the Problem Space

Before proposing solutions, Marissa focuses on understanding the environment in which users operate and the underlying causes of product challenges.

Research activities may include:

• user interviews  
• analytics reviews  
• support ticket analysis  
• stakeholder workshops  

These inputs help reveal the root causes behind user pain points and operational challenges.

---

## Synthesizing Research Insights

Raw research data must be translated into meaningful insights.

Marissa organizes research findings using synthesis techniques such as:

• affinity mapping  
• pattern identification  
• journey mapping  

This process helps teams identify recurring themes in user behavior and understand where friction occurs across the user experience.

---

## Defining Design Opportunities

Once patterns emerge from the research, opportunities for improvement begin to surface.

Marissa works with product teams to define design opportunities such as:

• simplifying complex workflows  
• improving navigation structures  
• reducing friction in key user tasks  

These opportunities help guide concept exploration and feature development.

---

## Translating Insights Into Design

Research insights directly inform the creation of design concepts and prototypes.

Design artifacts may include:

• user flows  
• wireframes  
• interaction models  
• system diagrams  

Each artifact reflects insights gathered during the research process and helps visualize potential solutions.

---

## Validating Through Testing

Design concepts are validated through user testing to ensure that proposed solutions address the identified problems.

Feedback from testing informs further refinements and helps teams confirm that design decisions remain aligned with real user needs.
