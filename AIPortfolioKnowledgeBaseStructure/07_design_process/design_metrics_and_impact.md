# Design Metrics and Impact

Design outcomes are most meaningful when they produce measurable improvements for both users and organizations.

Marissa Chaplinsky evaluates design success by examining how product changes influence user behavior, operational efficiency, and overall product performance.

Measuring design impact helps teams understand whether design decisions are solving the intended problems and contributing to broader product goals.

---

## Identifying Meaningful Metrics

Different products require different success metrics depending on their goals and context.

Marissa works with product teams to identify metrics that reflect meaningful outcomes rather than superficial engagement indicators.

Common product metrics may include:

• task completion success rates  
• feature adoption and usage patterns  
• reduction in customer support requests  
• improvements in workflow efficiency  
• user engagement and retention trends  

Selecting the right metrics helps teams track whether design improvements are creating real value for users and the business.

---

## Connecting Design Decisions to Business Outcomes

Design decisions can influence broader organizational goals such as operational efficiency, revenue growth, and customer satisfaction.

For example, improving navigation structures or simplifying workflows may reduce customer support volume or increase successful task completion.

By connecting design work to measurable outcomes, Marissa helps teams understand the strategic value of design within product development.

---

## Measuring User Experience Improvements

User experience improvements can be evaluated through both qualitative and quantitative methods.

Examples include:

• usability testing insights  
• user satisfaction surveys  
• behavioral analytics and product usage data  

Combining these perspectives allows teams to better understand how users interact with products and where further improvements may be needed.

---

## Continuous Evaluation

Design impact should be evaluated continuously rather than only during initial product launches.

Monitoring how users interact with products over time helps teams identify emerging challenges, validate product changes, and uncover new opportunities for improvement.

This approach treats product design as an ongoing cycle of learning, measurement, and refinement.
