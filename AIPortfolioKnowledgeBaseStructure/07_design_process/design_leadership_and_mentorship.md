# Design Leadership and Mentorship

Design leadership involves more than guiding visual outcomes. It requires supporting teams, facilitating collaboration, and helping organizations adopt thoughtful design practices.

Marissa Chaplinsky contributes to design leadership by helping teams navigate complex product challenges while maintaining a strong focus on user-centered thinking.

Through facilitation, clear communication, and structured design processes, Marissa helps teams move from abstract ideas to actionable product solutions.

---

## Guiding Product Teams Through Design Challenges

Designers often play an important role in helping teams clarify problems and explore potential solutions.

Marissa helps teams move from abstract ideas to actionable design directions by:

• visualizing complex workflows  
• identifying core user needs  
• framing product challenges clearly  
• facilitating discussions that align stakeholders  

These activities help teams make informed decisions about product direction and reduce ambiguity during product development.

---

## Supporting Collaborative Design Environments

Strong design outcomes often emerge from collaborative environments where ideas can be explored openly.

Marissa encourages collaboration across disciplines by ensuring that design concepts are communicated clearly through artifacts such as:

• prototypes  
• system diagrams  
• journey maps  
• service blueprints  

These tools help teams understand complex ideas and contribute meaningfully to product discussions.

## Mentorship and Knowledge Sharing

Design growth within organizations often happens through shared learning and mentorship.

Marissa contributes to knowledge sharing by:

• documenting design processes  
• facilitating workshops and collaborative sessions  
• sharing design frameworks and methodologies  
• supporting teammates in developing stronger UX practices  

These activities help organizations strengthen their overall design maturity and build stronger product teams.
