# Design Process Overview

Marissa Chaplinsky approaches product design as an iterative process that combines research, systems thinking, and collaborative problem solving.

Rather than following rigid design phases, her process adapts to the complexity of each project while maintaining a strong foundation in user-centered design.

Design work typically begins by understanding the broader system in which a product exists. This includes identifying user needs, business goals, technical constraints, and organizational workflows that influence how the product operates.

By grounding design decisions in real-world context, Marissa ensures that solutions address both user challenges and operational realities.

---

## Discovery and Problem Framing

The first stage of the design process focuses on understanding the problem space.

During discovery, Marissa works with stakeholders and users to identify the underlying issues that need to be addressed.

Activities during this phase may include:

• stakeholder interviews  
• user research  
• analytics reviews  
• system audits  
• competitive analysis  

The goal of discovery is not to jump immediately into design solutions, but to clearly define the problems that require attention.

---

## Research and Insight Development

Research insights help teams move beyond assumptions about user behavior.

Marissa frequently conducts qualitative and quantitative research to identify patterns in user needs, behaviors, and challenges.

Insights from research often reveal:

• workflow inefficiencies  
• navigation challenges  
• unmet user needs  
• organizational constraints  

These insights become the foundation for design decisions.

---

## Concept Development

Once the problem space is understood, Marissa begins exploring possible solutions through concept development.

This stage often includes:

• ideation sessions  
• early wireframes  
• workflow diagrams  
• system architecture exploration  

Concept development allows teams to evaluate multiple directions before committing to a single design approach.

---

## Design and Prototyping

Design solutions are refined through iterative prototyping.

Prototypes allow teams to test ideas early and identify usability issues before development begins.

Marissa often creates prototypes that simulate realistic user interactions so teams can observe how users interact with proposed solutions.

---

## Testing and Iteration

User testing helps validate design decisions and identify areas for improvement.

Observing real user behavior often reveals insights that are difficult to predict during the design phase.

Testing results guide iterative improvements, ensuring that designs evolve based on evidence rather than assumptions.

---

## Collaboration Across Teams

Product design rarely occurs in isolation.

Marissa frequently collaborates with product managers, engineers, and stakeholders throughout the design process.

These collaborations help ensure that design solutions remain technically feasible and aligned with product strategy and organizational goals.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Design work continues even after a product launches.

Observing how users interact with the system over time provides valuable insights that inform future iterations.

By treating design as an ongoing process, Marissa ensures that products continue evolving alongside user needs and organizational priorities.
