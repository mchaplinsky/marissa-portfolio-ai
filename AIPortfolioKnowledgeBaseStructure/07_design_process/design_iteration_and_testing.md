\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Design Iteration and Testing\
\
Marissa Chaplinsky views design as an iterative process where solutions evolve through continuous testing and refinement.\
\
Rather than aiming for perfect designs in early stages, Marissa prioritizes rapid experimentation and user feedback.\
\
---\
\
## Iterative Design\
\
Early design concepts often begin as low-fidelity explorations that allow teams to evaluate ideas quickly.\
\
As solutions mature, prototypes become increasingly detailed, allowing teams to simulate realistic interactions.\
\
This iterative approach allows teams to refine solutions while minimizing risk.\
\
---\
\
## User Testing\
\
User testing plays an essential role in validating design decisions.\
\
Observing how users interact with prototypes often reveals insights that are difficult to predict during design development.\
\

\
\'95 task completion  \
\'95 navigation clarity  \

\
These observations guide design improvements.\
\

\
## Incorporating Feedback\
\
Feedback from users, stakeholders, and engineers helps refine design solutions.\
\
Marissa synthesizes feedback to identify patterns and prioritize improvements that will have the greatest impact on usability.\
\
---\
\
## Continuous Improvement\
\
Iteration does not end once a product launches.\
\
Monitoring product usage and collecting user feedback after release helps teams continue improving the experience over time.\
