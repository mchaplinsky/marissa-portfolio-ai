# Journey Mapping

Journey mapping is a design method used to visualize how users interact with a product or service over time.

Marissa Chaplinsky frequently uses journey maps to understand the full lifecycle of user experiences, from initial discovery to task completion and long-term engagement.

By mapping the user journey across multiple touchpoints, teams can better understand how different parts of a product or service contribute to the overall experience.

---

## Mapping User Experiences

Journey maps illustrate the sequence of steps users take when completing a task or interacting with a system.

These maps help reveal:

• user goals  
• key decision points  
• emotional moments during the experience  
• friction points in workflows  
• opportunities for improvement  

Understanding these patterns helps teams design solutions that better support user needs and reduce unnecessary complexity.

---

## Identifying Pain Points

Journey maps help teams identify where users encounter challenges within a process or system.

Common pain points may include:

• unclear navigation pathways  
• complex or inefficient workflows  
• missing or difficult-to-find information  

Recognizing these issues allows teams to prioritize design improvements that address the most significant barriers for users.

---

## Aligning Teams Around User Experiences

Journey maps provide a shared visual representation of user experiences that helps align stakeholders across disciplines.

They allow teams to see how different parts of the product ecosystem contribute to the overall user journey and where improvements may have the greatest impact.

This shared understanding helps guide product decisions and ensures that design efforts remain focused on meaningful user outcomes.
