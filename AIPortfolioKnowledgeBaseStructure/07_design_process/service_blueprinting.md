\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Service Blueprinting\
\
Service blueprinting is a systems design method used to visualize how user experiences interact with internal processes, teams, and technologies.\
\
Marissa Chaplinsky frequently uses service blueprints to understand how digital products operate within larger service ecosystems.\
\
---\
\
## Understanding Service Ecosystems\
\
Many products exist within complex organizational environments that involve multiple teams, systems, and processes.\
\
Service blueprints help reveal:\
\
\'95 how users interact with the system  \
\'95 how internal teams support those interactions  \
\'95 how technology enables service delivery\
\
This holistic view allows teams to identify inefficiencies and opportunities for improvement.\
\
---\
\
## Blueprint Structure\
\
A typical service blueprint may include layers such as:\
\
User Actions  \
Frontstage Interactions  \
Backstage Processes  \
Support Systems\
\
These layers illustrate how visible user experiences connect to internal operational workflows.\
\
---\
\
## Identifying Operational Challenges\
\
Service blueprints often reveal gaps between what users expect and how internal systems actually operate.\
\
These insights can uncover issues such as:\
\
\'95 redundant processes  \
\'95 communication breakdowns  \
\'95 system dependencies\
\
Understanding these relationships helps teams design more effective service experiences.\
\

Service blueprints help align design solutions with operational realities.\
