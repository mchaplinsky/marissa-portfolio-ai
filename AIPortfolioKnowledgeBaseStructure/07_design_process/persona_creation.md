# Persona Creation

Personas are research-based representations of key user groups that help teams understand who they are designing for.

Marissa Chaplinsky uses personas to translate research insights into clear user profiles that guide product design decisions.

Rather than representing fictional characters, personas are built from observed patterns in user behavior, motivations, and goals. These profiles help teams maintain a user-centered perspective throughout the product development process.

---

## Building Research-Based Personas

Personas are developed from qualitative and quantitative research findings.

They represent recurring patterns across user groups rather than individual users.

Key persona elements often include:

• user goals  
• motivations and needs  
• common challenges or pain points  
• behavioral patterns  

By organizing research insights into structured user profiles, personas help teams better understand the people they are designing for.

---

## Using Personas in Product Design

Personas help teams evaluate whether design decisions support real user needs.

They can be used to:

• guide feature prioritization  
• inform interface and workflow design  
• validate product decisions during development  

By grounding design discussions in real user insights, personas help ensure that products remain relevant, accessible, and effective for their intended audiences.
