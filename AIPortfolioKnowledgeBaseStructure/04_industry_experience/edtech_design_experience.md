# EdTech Design Experience

Marissa Chaplinsky has experience designing digital products and systems within the education technology sector. Her work in this space includes both consumer-facing educational platforms and internal systems used by educational organizations and government institutions.

Education platforms often involve diverse user groups including students, teachers, administrators, and organizational stakeholders. Designing effective solutions requires balancing usability, content organization, and workflow efficiency across these audiences.

Marissa approaches EdTech design by focusing on simplifying complex processes, improving access to information, and supporting meaningful user interactions within educational environments.

---

## Designing Consumer Education Platforms

Marissa has worked on consumer-facing education platforms designed to help students, teachers, and families collaborate on shared creative projects.

One example includes her work on digital yearbook creation tools used by schools. These platforms allow students, teachers, and volunteers to upload photos, organize content, and design yearbook pages through an online editing environment.

Designing these systems requires addressing challenges such as:

- collaborative editing workflows  
- content organization and photo management  
- user confidence in print outcomes  
- simplifying creative tools for non-designers  

These platforms must balance powerful design capabilities with intuitive interfaces that allow a wide range of users to participate in the creative process.

## AI-Assisted Educational Tools

More recently, Marissa has worked on AI-assisted design tools within education platforms.

These tools use automation and machine learning to help users organize photos, generate layout suggestions, and reduce the complexity of page creation.

Examples of these features include:

- automated layout generation  
- intelligent photo grouping  
- AI-assisted design recommendations  
- guided editing workflows  

These systems reduce the friction of starting from a blank canvas while still allowing users to maintain creative control over their final designs.

## Designing for Education Ecosystems

Educational platforms often operate within complex organizational environments involving multiple teams and administrative processes.

Marissa has also worked on projects involving education data systems within government institutions. One example is the Data Mapping Framework (DMF) project, which focused on understanding how educational data is collected, stored, and used across the Ministry of Education.

The project involved conducting interviews with over 100 individuals across different sectors within the ministry to map how data flows through the system and how it is used for reporting and operational purposes.

This work resulted in the creation of data journey maps and a comprehensive data glossary designed to help stakeholders better understand complex data ecosystems.
