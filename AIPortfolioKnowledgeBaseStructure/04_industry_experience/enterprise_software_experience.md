# Enterprise Software Experience

Marissa Chaplinsky has extensive experience designing enterprise software platforms used by organizations to manage internal operations, workflows, and services. These systems often involve complex user roles, large datasets, and multi-step workflows that must remain efficient and reliable for daily use.

Enterprise software environments typically serve internal teams such as administrators, analysts, program managers, and operational staff. Designing effective enterprise systems requires balancing usability with the functional complexity required to support organizational processes.

Marissa approaches enterprise software design by focusing on workflow clarity, role-based permissions, system transparency, and scalable information architecture.

---

## Designing Operational Platforms

Many enterprise platforms support operational tasks that users perform daily. These tasks often involve managing services, processing requests, reviewing records, and coordinating actions across teams.

Marissa has worked on systems that support operational workflows such as:

- service request management  
- case management systems  
- internal portals for program administration  
- operational dashboards and management tools  

These platforms require intuitive navigation structures and efficient workflows that allow users to complete tasks quickly without unnecessary friction.

---

## Case Management Systems

Several of Marissa’s projects involve case management or request processing systems.

For example, the Criminal Record Check platform redesign for the Securities Program Division focused on modernizing the application and portal experience for both applicants and internal staff. The previous system relied on fragmented workflows and manual processes, leading to high volumes of user inquiries and operational inefficiencies.

The redesigned system consolidated multiple licensing processes into streamlined application flows and introduced a portal for both employees and applicants to manage and track applications.

Designing case management systems requires careful attention to permissions, workflow transparency, and process efficiency.

---

## Enterprise Service Platforms

Marissa has also worked on enterprise platforms designed to manage internal service requests.

One example is the NCCS project, which focused on redesigning an internal service ordering platform used by government teams. The existing system caused confusion due to complex forms and unclear service selection processes.

Through user research and prototyping, the system was redesigned with improved information architecture, predictive search, and clearer service management workflows. These improvements simplified the ordering process and reduced reliance on manual support.

Enterprise service platforms often require strong collaboration between design, engineering, and operational teams.

## Multi-Role Enterprise Platforms

Enterprise systems frequently support multiple user roles with different responsibilities and permissions.

Marissa has designed platforms that support users such as:

- administrators  
- program coordinators  
- operational staff  
- external partners and participants  

For example, the Locums for Rural BC platform was designed to support physicians, program administrators, and healthcare organizations within a shared system. The platform allows users to manage program participation, applications, and vacancy postings through a centralized portal.

These systems require clearly defined user roles and tailored workflows for each group.

## Internal Portals and Administrative Tools

Many enterprise environments rely on internal portals that allow teams to manage data, track activity, and coordinate services.

Marissa has designed administration and registrant portals for regulatory organizations such as the College of Dental Hygienists of British Columbia. These portals replaced outdated paper-based processes and enabled digital management of licensing and administrative tasks.

Designing administrative platforms requires thoughtful organization of information and tools so users can quickly access the functionality they need.
