# B2B Product Design Experience

Marissa Chaplinsky has extensive experience designing B2B digital products that support organizations, operational teams, and professional users. These platforms often involve complex workflows, multi-role environments, and systems that support critical day-to-day operations.

B2B product environments differ from consumer products in that they prioritize efficiency, workflow clarity, and information transparency. Users rely on these systems to complete professional tasks quickly and accurately, making usability and reliability essential.

Marissa approaches B2B product design by focusing on workflow optimization, role-based experiences, and scalable information architecture that supports complex operational needs.

---

## Designing Tools for Professional Users

B2B platforms are typically used by professionals performing specialized tasks within organizations.

Marissa has designed systems used by professionals such as:

- administrators  
- program coordinators  
- healthcare professionals  
- government staff  
- operational teams  

These users often rely on software to complete mission-critical tasks. Interfaces must therefore prioritize efficiency, clarity, and accuracy rather than exploration or entertainment.

Designing for professional users requires careful attention to workflow optimization and information structure.

---

## Multi-Role Platforms and Permission Structures

Many B2B systems support multiple user types within the same platform.

Marissa frequently designs systems with role-based permissions that allow different users to access tools and information relevant to their responsibilities.

Examples of user roles within B2B platforms include:

- administrators managing systems and permissions  
- operational staff completing workflows  
- external partners interacting with services  
- internal program managers overseeing processes  

Designing role-based experiences ensures users see only the tools and data relevant to their work.

---

## Workflow-Centered Design

Unlike consumer applications, B2B systems often revolve around completing structured tasks.

Marissa focuses on designing workflows that allow users to move efficiently through multi-step processes such as:

- submitting applications  
- managing service requests  
- reviewing records or cases  
- coordinating activities across departments  

These workflows are often supported by structured tools such as dashboards, forms, and data management interfaces.

---

## Designing for Operational Efficiency

Many B2B platforms are used daily by internal teams, meaning even small usability improvements can significantly impact productivity.

Marissa designs interfaces that support:

- quick access to commonly used tasks  
- clear navigation structures  
- searchable data and filtering tools  
- transparent system feedback  

Improving operational efficiency helps organizations reduce manual work and improve service delivery.

B2B platforms often involve complex technical systems and integrations.

Marissa regularly collaborates with:

- product managers

## Designing Within Organizational Constraints

B2B systems often operate within environments that include strict requirements such as security, permissions, compliance, and operational procedures.

Marissa designs solutions that account for these constraints while still improving usability and system transparency.

Understanding how software fits within broader organizational processes is essential for successful B2B product design.
