# Healthcare Design Experience

Marissa Chaplinsky has extensive experience designing digital platforms within healthcare and regulated service environments. These systems often involve sensitive user data, complex operational workflows, and diverse user groups including healthcare professionals, administrators, patients, and the public.

Her healthcare design work focuses on improving access to services, simplifying complex processes, and ensuring that platforms remain usable, accessible, and trustworthy for users navigating important health-related information.

Healthcare platforms frequently require balancing usability, compliance considerations, and operational complexity. Marissa approaches these projects by combining user research, service design thinking, and systems-level design to create solutions that support both end users and internal teams.

---

## Designing for Complex Healthcare Ecosystems

Healthcare systems often involve interconnected platforms that serve multiple user groups with different responsibilities and workflows.

Marissa has designed systems that support:

- healthcare professionals  
- program administrators  
- patients and families  
- regulatory organizations  
- internal support teams  

These environments require thoughtful information architecture, clear workflows, and strong permission structures so users can access the information and tools relevant to their roles.

Designing within these ecosystems requires a strong understanding of operational processes as well as user needs.

## Mental Health Platforms

Marissa has worked closely with mental health organizations to improve digital access to information and services.

Her work with the Canadian Mental Health Association (CMHA) involved conducting a comprehensive audit of multiple platforms and identifying usability issues contributing to declining program sign-ups. The work included reviewing analytics data, auditing accessibility, evaluating user pathways, and redesigning the information architecture to improve access to mental health resources.

This work helped identify structural challenges within the platform and informed recommendations for improving usability and engagement.

---

## Mental Health Rights and Public Information Platforms

In another CMHA project, Marissa designed a new public-facing platform focused on providing accessible information related to involuntary treatment and mental health rights.

This project involved creating a new website alongside internal portals that allowed staff members, rights advisors, and facilities to manage communications and workflows. The platform was designed to present complex information in a clear and accessible format for individuals navigating sensitive mental health situations.

The work required balancing clarity of information, accessibility standards, and internal operational needs within a tight project timeline.

## Healthcare Workforce Platforms

Marissa has also designed platforms that support healthcare professionals and operational programs.

One example is the Locums for Rural BC (LRBC) platform, which supports physicians participating in rural healthcare programs. The platform allows physicians and administrators to manage vacancies, applications, and program participation while providing a centralized hub for program information.

Designing this platform required mapping complex workflows across multiple user groups including physicians, employers, administrators, and program coordinators.

---

## Healthcare Administration Systems

In addition to public-facing platforms, Marissa has designed internal healthcare administration systems.

For example, the CDHBC project involved designing administration and registrant portals for the College of Dental Hygienists of British Columbia. The goal was to replace outdated paper-based workflows with a modern digital portal that allowed administrators and registrants to manage licensing and program interactions more efficiently.

These systems required careful attention to data privacy, usability, and workflow efficiency.

## Designing for Accessibility and Inclusivity

Healthcare platforms must remain accessible to a wide range of users with varying levels of digital literacy and accessibility needs.

Marissa incorporates accessibility best practices into healthcare platforms by evaluating:

- readability and typography  
- color contrast and visual hierarchy  
- navigation clarity  
- content organization  

These considerations help ensure healthcare information remains accessible and usable for diverse audiences.

---

## Service Design in Healthcare Systems

Many healthcare platforms exist within broader service ecosystems involving multiple departments, organizations, and operational workflows.

Marissa frequently uses service design methods such as:

- service blueprints  
- workflow mapping  
- journey mapping  
- stakeholder workshops  

These tools help teams understand how different parts of the system interact and identify opportunities for improving service delivery.
