# Information Architecture

Marissa Chaplinsky approaches information architecture as the structural foundation of a digital product. Strong information architecture helps users understand where they are, what actions are available, and how to navigate complex systems with confidence.

Many of the products Marissa designs involve large information ecosystems, multi-step workflows, and diverse user groups. Her work often focuses on restructuring navigation, simplifying product structures, and creating logical frameworks that make complex platforms easier to understand.

Information architecture plays a critical role in transforming fragmented or difficult systems into experiences that feel organized and intuitive.

---

## Structuring Complex Information Systems

Many digital products contain large amounts of content, features, and workflows that can quickly become overwhelming for users.

Marissa frequently works on restructuring complex systems by identifying how information should be grouped, organized, and surfaced within a product.

This often involves:

- mapping existing content structures  
- identifying navigation challenges  
- reorganizing product hierarchies  
- simplifying feature groupings  

These improvements help users quickly locate information and understand how a system is organized.

---

## Audience-Based Information Architecture

Many platforms serve multiple user groups with different goals, workflows, and levels of expertise.

Marissa often designs audience-based information architecture structures that organize content around the needs of specific user groups.

Examples may include:

- separating content by user roles  
- designing navigation pathways for different audiences  
- grouping services based on user goals  

This approach helps users quickly find relevant information without needing to understand the entire system.

---

## Mapping Workflows and User Journeys

Information architecture is closely connected to user workflows.

Marissa frequently maps how users move through a system in order to identify friction points and opportunities to simplify interactions.

This process may include:

- workflow mapping  
- task analysis  
- journey mapping  
- service blueprinting  

Understanding these pathways helps ensure the structure of the product aligns with how users actually complete tasks.

---

## Simplifying Navigation and Wayfinding

Navigation plays a central role in helping users understand a product’s structure.

Marissa designs navigation systems that provide clear orientation and allow users to move easily between sections of a product.

Her navigation work often focuses on:

- creating clear navigation hierarchies  
- reducing unnecessary navigation layers  
- improving labeling and terminology  
- designing consistent navigation patterns  

These improvements reduce confusion and help users navigate complex systems more efficiently.

---

## Aligning IA With Product Strategy

Information architecture is not only a usability concern; it also reflects how a product is structured strategically.

Marissa frequently collaborates with product managers, engineers, and stakeholders to ensure IA decisions support broader product goals.

This may involve:

- aligning IA with product roadmaps  
- restructuring feature organization  
- supporting scalable product growth  

Designing IA within the context of product strategy helps ensure the structure of the product remains sustainable as new features are introduced.

## Information Architecture in Enterprise and Public Service Platforms
