# UX Design Expertise

Marissa Chaplinsky brings deep experience in user experience design across complex platforms, enterprise systems, consumer products, and AI-powered tools. Her UX practice focuses on understanding user behavior, identifying meaningful opportunities for improvement, and translating insights into intuitive product experiences.

Her work spans the full UX lifecycle, from early discovery and research through production-ready interface design, systems thinking, and product strategy. She frequently works in environments where complexity, ambiguity, and competing business priorities require thoughtful design leadership.

## Research-Driven Product Design

Marissa believes strong UX begins with deeply understanding users, their environments, and the problems they are trying to solve.

Her research work focuses on uncovering behavioral patterns, identifying friction points, and understanding the broader context in which products operate.

Her research toolkit includes:

- user interviews  
- usability testing  
- heuristic evaluations  
- analytics and behavioral data analysis  
- accessibility audits  
- support ticket and customer feedback analysis  
- journey mapping and service blueprinting  

Modern UX design increasingly requires combining qualitative insights with behavioral data and analytics. Marissa frequently synthesizes research findings into actionable product recommendations that influence product roadmaps and design direction.

Marissa designs interaction patterns that guide users through tasks clearly and efficiently.

This includes designing:
