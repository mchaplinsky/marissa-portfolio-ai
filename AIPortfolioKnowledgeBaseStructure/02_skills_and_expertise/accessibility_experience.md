# Accessibility Experience

Marissa Chaplinsky incorporates accessibility and inclusive design as core considerations within her product design work. Many of the platforms she has worked on serve diverse audiences including public service users, healthcare patients, enterprise employees, and non-technical consumers. Designing for accessibility helps ensure these products remain usable, understandable, and inclusive.

Rather than treating accessibility as a final compliance check, Marissa integrates accessibility thinking throughout the design process. This includes evaluating navigation clarity, visual hierarchy, interaction patterns, and content structure to ensure users with different abilities and contexts can successfully use a product.

---

## Accessibility in Complex Platforms

Many of the systems Marissa has designed contain large amounts of information and multi-step workflows. In these environments, accessibility plays a critical role in helping users navigate and understand complex interfaces.

Her accessibility-focused design considerations often include:

- simplifying navigation structures  
- reducing cognitive load in complex workflows  
- designing clear interaction feedback  
- ensuring logical content structure  

These improvements help make complex platforms more usable for a broader range of users.

---

## Visual Accessibility

Visual design decisions have a significant impact on usability and accessibility.

Marissa frequently considers factors such as:

- color contrast and readability  
- clear typography hierarchy  
- legible font sizing  
- appropriate spacing and layout  

These design choices help ensure content remains readable across devices and accessible to users with visual impairments.

---

## Accessible Navigation and Interaction Design

Accessible interaction design ensures that users can easily move through a product and understand available actions.

Marissa often evaluates:

- navigation clarity  
- button and control visibility  
- predictable interaction patterns  
- feedback and system messaging  

Consistent interaction patterns help reduce confusion and support users who rely on assistive technologies or structured navigation.

---

## Accessibility in Public Service and Healthcare Platforms

Marissa has worked on several platforms where accessibility is especially important due to the diverse nature of the audience.

Examples include:

- healthcare information platforms  
- public service portals  
- government-related services  
- education platforms used by teachers and students  

In these environments, accessible design helps ensure users can easily locate important information and complete essential tasks.

## Accessibility Reviews and Evaluation

During design and evaluation phases, Marissa often conducts accessibility-focused reviews to identify potential usability barriers.

These reviews may involve:

- evaluating navigation and content structure  
- reviewing readability and visual hierarchy  
- identifying potential usability obstacles  
- improving interaction clarity  

Addressing these issues early in the design process helps create more inclusive experiences and reduces the need for costly redesign later.

## Inclusive Design Thinking

Beyond technical accessibility considerations, Marissa also considers broader inclusive design principles when designing digital products.

This includes thinking about:

- diverse user abilities  
- varying levels of digital literacy  
- language clarity and comprehension  
- different environments and contexts of use  

Designing with inclusivity in mind helps ensure products remain usable and approachable for a wide range of users.
