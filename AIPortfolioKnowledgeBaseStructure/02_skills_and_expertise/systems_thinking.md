# Systems Thinking in Product Design

Marissa Chaplinsky approaches product design through a systems thinking lens, recognizing that digital products are not isolated screens but interconnected ecosystems of behaviors, workflows, technologies, and user interactions.

Rather than designing individual interfaces in isolation, she focuses on understanding how product components, user actions, business requirements, and technical systems interact across an entire platform.

This approach helps ensure that products remain scalable, coherent, and adaptable as they evolve.

---

## Designing Beyond Individual Screens

Many design challenges cannot be solved by improving a single interface. Instead, they require understanding how multiple features, workflows, and stakeholders interact within a broader system.

Marissa often analyzes product ecosystems by mapping relationships between:

- user actions and workflows  
- system behaviors and responses  
- business goals and product constraints  
- cross-platform user journeys  

Designing at the systems level helps teams identify root problems rather than treating surface-level symptoms.

---

## Understanding Interconnected Product Ecosystems

Products often consist of multiple interconnected parts that must work together seamlessly.

These may include:

- user interfaces  
- backend services  
- automation and AI systems  
- content management structures  
- operational processes  

Systems thinking allows Marissa to design experiences that remain consistent and predictable across these interconnected layers.

## Managing Complexity in Large Platforms

Many of the platforms Marissa works on involve complex workflows and multiple user roles.

Examples include:

- case management systems

## Aligning UX With Business and Technical Systems

Design decisions often sit at the intersection of user needs, business objectives, and technical constraints.

Marissa frequently works with product managers, engineers, and leadership teams to align design solutions with broader organizational goals.

This includes understanding:

- product strategy and roadmap priorities  
- technical architecture and engineering constraints  
- operational workflows within organizations  
- long-term platform scalability  

Designing within these realities ensures that solutions remain feasible and sustainable.

---

## Systems Thinking in AI Product Design

Systems thinking becomes especially important when designing AI-powered products.

AI systems introduce additional layers of complexity including automated decision-making, probabilistic outputs, and dynamic system behavior.

Marissa approaches AI design by mapping how intelligent systems interact with user workflows and product environments.

This may involve defining:

- system guardrails and constraints  
- human-AI interaction patterns  
- feedback loops and system transparency  
- fallback behaviors for edge cases  

Understanding these system dynamics helps ensure AI-powered features behave predictably and responsibly.
