# AI Design Strategy

Marissa Chaplinsky approaches AI product design as a systems problem rather than a feature problem. Designing AI products requires defining how intelligence behaves within a product ecosystem while maintaining usability, reliability, and user trust.

AI systems introduce new design challenges compared to traditional software. Rather than simply designing interfaces, designers must define system behavior, user expectations, and the boundaries between automation and human control.

Marissa’s AI design strategy focuses on balancing automation with user agency, ensuring that intelligent systems assist users without removing their ability to understand or influence outcomes.

---

## Identifying Meaningful AI Opportunities

Not every problem requires AI. One of the most important aspects of AI product strategy is identifying where automation provides meaningful value for users.

Marissa evaluates AI opportunities by examining:

- repetitive workflows that could be automated  
- areas where users struggle with blank-canvas creation  
- processes involving large volumes of data or content  
- decision-support scenarios where suggestions are useful  

This evaluation helps determine whether AI should **generate, recommend, or assist**.

---

## Designing AI as a Collaborative System

AI products work best when positioned as collaborative systems rather than fully autonomous tools.

Marissa designs AI experiences that:

- generate suggestions rather than final decisions  
- allow users to edit and refine outputs  
- provide multiple options instead of a single answer  

This approach encourages exploration while preserving user control.

Human–AI collaboration allows intelligent systems to accelerate workflows while still respecting the user's role in decision making and creativity.

---

## Structuring AI System Behavior

AI systems require clearly defined behavior rules to ensure consistent and predictable outcomes.

Marissa often collaborates with engineering teams to define:

- system triggers  
- decision logic  
- output formatting  
- fallback states  

These behavioral structures help ensure the AI behaves consistently across different scenarios and prevents unpredictable interactions.

---

## Balancing Automation and Control

Successful AI systems must balance automation with user agency.

Too much automation can create confusion and mistrust, while too little automation reduces the benefits of AI.

Marissa designs AI interactions that:

- automate repetitive tasks  
- keep users in control of final decisions  
- provide visibility into system behavior  

This balance creates systems that feel helpful rather than intrusive.

---

## Designing AI for Real Product Environments

AI systems must operate within real product constraints such as technical limitations, data quality, and operational requirements.

Marissa approaches AI product design with an awareness of:

- system limitations  
- edge cases  
- user expectations  
- organizational constraints  

Designing AI systems within these constraints helps ensure they remain practical, reliable, and scalable in production environments.

---

## Integrating AI Into Existing Workflows

Introducing AI into a product should improve existing workflows rather than disrupt them.

Marissa focuses on identifying moments within the user journey where intelligent assistance can reduce friction or accelerate tasks.

Examples include:

- generating starting points for complex work  
- assisting with repetitive organization tasks  
- providing contextual recommendations  

By integrating AI into familiar workflows, users can adopt new capabilities without needing to completely change how they work.
