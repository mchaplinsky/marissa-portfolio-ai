# AI Trust, Transparency, and Control

Trust is one of the most important factors in the success of AI-powered products.

Users must feel confident that the system behaves reliably and that they maintain control over outcomes.

Marissa Chaplinsky focuses on designing AI experiences that prioritize trust, transparency, and user control. These principles help ensure that intelligent systems remain understandable, predictable, and supportive rather than confusing or opaque.

---

## Transparency

Transparency helps users understand how and why the system produces certain results.

AI systems can sometimes feel opaque if users cannot interpret system behavior. Designing transparent interactions helps users understand what the system is doing and why.

Examples include:

• explaining AI-generated suggestions  
• highlighting the factors influencing outputs  
• clearly labeling automated results  
• providing contextual system feedback  

These signals help users interpret AI outputs and build confidence in system behavior.

---

## User Control

AI systems should support user agency rather than remove it.

Marissa designs experiences that allow users to influence and refine automated outcomes rather than simply accepting them.

Control mechanisms may include:

• editing AI-generated outputs  
• selecting between multiple system suggestions  
• adjusting inputs to refine results  
• disabling or overriding automated behaviors  

These interactions ensure that users remain the final decision-makers in AI-assisted workflows.

---

## Building Trust Over Time

Trust in AI systems develops gradually as users observe consistent and reliable system behavior.

Predictable interactions, transparent messaging, and clear system boundaries help users understand how the AI behaves and what they can expect from it.

As users gain familiarity with the system, they become more comfortable relying on AI suggestions while still maintaining control over final outcomes.

Designing for long-term trust helps ensure that AI features are adopted and integrated into user workflows.
