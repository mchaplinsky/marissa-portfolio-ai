# Human-in-the-Loop Design

Human-in-the-loop design ensures that users remain actively involved in AI-driven workflows.

Rather than replacing human decision-making, intelligent systems assist users by providing suggestions, automation, and guidance while preserving human oversight.

Marissa Chaplinsky frequently designs AI-powered experiences that keep users involved in critical decisions, ensuring that automation enhances productivity without removing user agency.

---

## Maintaining User Control

Human-in-the-loop systems allow users to remain responsible for final outcomes.

These systems often allow users to:

• review AI-generated outputs  
• modify or refine results  
• accept or reject system suggestions  

By keeping decision-making authority with the user, these designs help maintain trust in automated systems.

---

## Supporting Creative Workflows

Human oversight is especially important in creative environments.

AI systems can help generate ideas, organize content, or provide design suggestions, but users ultimately decide how those ideas evolve.

Marissa often designs collaborative workflows where AI provides starting points while users guide the final result.

This relationship allows AI to accelerate creative work without diminishing human authorship.

---

## Preventing Over-Automation

Fully automated systems can create confusion or mistrust when users cannot understand how results were produced.

Human-in-the-loop systems help prevent this by ensuring that users remain aware of system behavior and can intervene when necessary.

Designing AI with this balance helps maintain transparency while still benefiting from automation.
