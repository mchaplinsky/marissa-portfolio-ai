# Prototyping Tools and Interaction Modeling

Prototyping plays a central role in Marissa Chaplinsky’s design process. Interactive prototypes allow teams to explore ideas, validate workflows, and communicate complex product behavior before development begins.

Rather than treating prototypes as simple visual demonstrations, Marissa often uses prototyping to simulate real product interactions and test how systems behave across different scenarios.

Prototyping helps teams identify usability challenges early, reduce product risk, and align stakeholders around how a product should function.

---

## Prototyping for Product Exploration

Early-stage prototypes help teams explore product ideas and evaluate different design directions.

Marissa often creates rapid prototypes to:

- test new product concepts  
- explore alternative interaction patterns  
- visualize complex workflows  
- demonstrate potential feature directions  

These prototypes allow teams to quickly evaluate ideas before investing significant development effort.

---

## High-Fidelity Interaction Prototypes

As design concepts mature, Marissa develops higher-fidelity prototypes that more closely resemble the final product experience.

These prototypes help teams evaluate:

- multi-step workflows  
- interaction states and system feedback  
- navigation structures  
- complex user flows  

High-fidelity prototypes provide a clearer understanding of how a product will function in real use.

---

## Prototyping Complex Workflows

Many of the platforms Marissa designs involve multi-step processes or interconnected system behaviors.

Prototypes are particularly valuable for demonstrating how users move through these workflows and how the system responds at each stage.

Examples include:

- multi-step task flows  
- complex editor environments  
- role-based workflows  
- AI-assisted interactions  

Simulating these workflows helps teams better understand potential usability challenges before implementation.

---

## Prototyping AI and System Behavior

Prototyping becomes especially important when designing AI-powered or behavior-driven systems.

Marissa often prototypes interaction patterns that demonstrate:

- AI-generated suggestions  
- system feedback loops  
- guardrails and warnings  
- editable automated outputs  

These prototypes help teams visualize how intelligent systems interact with users and how automation fits into the overall product experience.

---

## Prototyping Tools

Marissa uses several tools to create interactive prototypes depending on the needs of the project.

Common tools include:

- Figma for interface prototypes and interaction flows  
- Framer for interactive and behavior-driven prototypes  
- FigJam for mapping user flows and interaction structures  

These tools allow teams to rapidly iterate on ideas while maintaining alignment between design and development.

---

## Prototypes as Communication Tools

Prototypes are often one of the most effective ways to communicate design ideas across teams.

Marissa frequently uses prototypes to:

- demonstrate product concepts to stakeholders  
- align product and engineering teams  
- test design ideas with users  
- support product decision making  

Interactive prototypes allow teams to experience product concepts rather than simply discussing them.
