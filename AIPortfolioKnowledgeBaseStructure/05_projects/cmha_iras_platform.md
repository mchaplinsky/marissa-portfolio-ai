# CMHA IRAS Platform

## Project Overview
The IRAS platform was designed to provide accessible online resources related to involuntary mental health treatment while also introducing internal portals that support communication between staff, healthcare facilities, and rights advisors.

## Industry
Mental Health / Healthcare

## Organization
Canadian Mental Health Association (CMHA)

## My Role
Lead UX Designer

## Project Scope
Design a new public-facing website and internal portals supporting the Independent Rights Advice Service (IRAS) program.

## Problem
The IRAS department required a platform that could present complex mental health rights information clearly for the public while also supporting internal communication and operational workflows between staff, advisors, and healthcare facilities.

## Users
- Patients and families  
- Rights advisors  
- CMHA staff  
- Healthcare facilities  

## Research Methods
- Stakeholder workshops  
- Iterative design reviews  
- Internal feedback sessions  

## Design Strategy
Create a clear, accessible public platform for mental health rights information supported by internal administrative portals that facilitate communication and operational coordination.

## Key UX Decisions
- Dedicated IRAS branding system  
- Simplified navigation for sensitive and complex content  
- Role-based internal portals supporting different staff workflows  

## Systems Thinking
The platform needed to support two interconnected environments:

- a public information platform designed for accessibility and clarity  
- internal portals supporting staff coordination and case-related communication  

Designing the system required balancing public usability with internal operational workflows.

## Accessibility Considerations
- Clear content hierarchy for sensitive information  
- Accessible navigation and labeling  
- Readable typography and visual structure  

## Deliverables
- Design system and branding framework  
- High-fidelity prototypes  
- Internal portal wireframes  

## Tools Used

## Business Impact
- Improved public access to critical mental health rights information  
- Reduced confusion around involuntary treatment processes  
- Created structured communication workflows between advisors, facilities, and CMHA staff  

## Impact

- Introduced new digital workflows supporting staff coordination and case-related communication
