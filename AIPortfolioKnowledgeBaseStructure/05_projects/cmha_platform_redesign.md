# CMHA Mental Health Platform Redesign

## Project Overview
The Canadian Mental Health Association (CMHA) project focused on identifying usability issues across several CMHA digital platforms and redesigning the information architecture to improve accessibility and user engagement.

## Industry
Mental Health / Healthcare

## Organization
Canadian Mental Health Association

## My Role
Senior UX Designer

## Project Scope
Conduct a comprehensive UX audit across multiple CMHA websites and digital platforms to identify barriers preventing users from signing up for programs and accessing mental health resources.

## Problem
CMHA experienced declining program sign-ups and suspected usability issues within their digital ecosystem. Users struggled to find relevant resources and navigate between multiple websites that served different programs and services.

## Users
- Mental health program participants  
- CMHA staff  
- Public users seeking mental health resources  

## Research Methods
- Website usability audits  
- Google Analytics analysis  
- User interviews  
- Accessibility reviews  

## Design Strategy
Improve the information architecture and navigation structure so users could more easily locate mental health programs, services, and educational resources across the CMHA ecosystem.

## Key UX Decisions
- Future-state site map redesign  
- Improved navigation structure  
- Accessibility-focused design improvements  

## Systems Thinking
The project required analyzing multiple interconnected CMHA websites and identifying structural issues across the broader ecosystem. Rather than focusing on individual pages, the work examined how users moved between platforms and how information was organized across the system.

## Deliverables

- UX and accessibility design recommendations
