# LRBC Rural Physician Platform

## Project Overview
The Locums for Rural BC (LRBC) platform was designed to support rural healthcare programs by helping physicians and administrators manage locum placements across British Columbia. The system allows healthcare organizations to post physician vacancies, coordinate placements, and manage program participation through a centralized digital platform.

The goal of the platform was to simplify how rural healthcare programs connect physicians with communities that require temporary medical coverage.

## Project Scope
Design a digital platform that allows physicians, healthcare organizations, and program administrators to manage locum placements, applications, and participation within rural healthcare programs.

## Problem
Healthcare organizations across rural communities struggled to efficiently coordinate physician placements. Existing processes relied on fragmented systems and manual coordination, making it difficult to manage program participation and communicate placement opportunities.

This created challenges such as:

- difficulty matching physicians with available placements  
- limited visibility into program participation  
- inefficient administrative coordination  
- reliance on manual communication and tracking  

## Users
- Physicians seeking locum placements  
- Healthcare administrators managing vacancies  
- Program coordinators overseeing participation  

## Research Methods
- Stakeholder interviews  
- Journey mapping

## Design Strategy
Design a centralized platform that improves visibility into rural healthcare placements while simplifying the administrative workflows required to manage program participation.

The platform focused on creating role-based experiences that allow each user group to quickly access the tools and information relevant to their responsibilities.

## Key UX Decisions
- Centralized vacancy listings for rural healthcare placements  
- Physician profiles and application workflows  
- Role-based dashboards for administrators and physicians  
- Clear program participation management tools  

These decisions helped simplify the process of matching physicians with communities that require temporary coverage.

## Systems Thinking
The platform needed to support multiple stakeholders across a complex healthcare ecosystem. Physicians, healthcare organizations, and program administrators all interact with the system in different ways.

Designing the platform required mapping these workflows and ensuring that each role had access to the appropriate tools, information, and actions within the system.
