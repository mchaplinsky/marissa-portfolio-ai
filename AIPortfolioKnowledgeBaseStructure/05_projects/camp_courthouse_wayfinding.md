# CAMP Courthouse Wayfinding System

## Project Overview
The CAMP project examined wayfinding challenges across courthouses in British Columbia and designed a scalable signage system to improve navigation for visitors and staff.

## Industry
Government / Public Infrastructure

## Organization
BC Ministry of the Attorney General

## My Role
Senior UX Designer

## Project Scope
Research and design of a universal wayfinding system across 89 courthouses in British Columbia.

## Problem
Visitors frequently struggled to navigate courthouse environments, which resulted in staff regularly interrupting their work to provide directions and assistance.

## Users
- Public visitors  
- Court staff  
- Security personnel  

## Research Methods
- Site visits  
- Wayfinding audits  
- User interviews  
- Best-practice research  

## Design Strategy
Develop a universal wayfinding framework that could scale across courthouses of different sizes and layouts while maintaining consistent signage and navigation patterns.

## Deliverables
- Wayfinding guidelines  
- Prototype signage system  
- User testing and evaluation  

## Tools Used
- Mural  
- Research frameworks  

## Impact
- Improved visitor navigation across courthouse environments  
- Reduced operational interruptions for courthouse staff
