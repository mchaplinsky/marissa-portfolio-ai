# TreeRing AI Assistant

## Project Overview
The TreeRing AI Assistant is an intelligent conversational interface designed to help users navigate the yearbook creation platform. The assistant provides contextual guidance, answers user questions, and helps users complete complex tasks throughout the yearbook design process.

The goal of the assistant was to reduce user confusion, improve platform onboarding, and provide real-time guidance while users design and manage yearbooks.

## Industry
Education Technology / AI-Assisted Products

## Organization
TreeRing

## My Role
Lead Product Designer

## Project Scope
Design an AI-powered assistant integrated within the yearbook creation platform that supports users through natural language interactions, guided workflows, and contextual product education.

The assistant needed to support a wide range of users including teachers, volunteers, and students who may have limited design or technical experience.

## Problem
Many users struggled to understand how to navigate the yearbook platform and complete complex tasks such as designing layouts, managing photos, and organizing content. These challenges often resulted in confusion, support requests, and incomplete projects.

Key challenges included:

- difficulty learning how to use the design platform  
- confusion around layout tools and editing workflows  
- reliance on customer support for guidance  
- lack of contextual help within the platform  

## Users

- Parent volunteers contributing content  
- Students participating in yearbook creation  
- School administrators overseeing projects  

## Research Methods
- User interviews with yearbook creators  
- Support ticket analysis

## Design Strategy
Design a conversational AI assistant that acts as a contextual guide throughout the yearbook creation process. The assistant was intended to reduce friction by providing users with immediate answers and step-by-step guidance directly within the product.

The system needed to support both simple informational questions and more complex guidance related to platform workflows.

## Key Features

### Natural Language Assistance
Users can ask questions in plain language about how to use the platform, such as how to upload photos, design layouts, or manage page content.

The assistant interprets user intent and provides clear responses that help users move forward in their workflow.

### Guided Workflows
The assistant helps users complete multi-step tasks by providing step-by-step instructions directly within the platform interface.

Examples include:

- creating new yearbook pages  
- organizing photos into layouts  
- managing page content and edits  

### Product Education
The assistant acts as an educational tool that helps users learn how the platform works over time.

Rather than forcing users to search through help documentation, the assistant provides contextual explanations when users need them.

## Design Focus

### Trust
Users needed to feel confident that the assistant was providing reliable guidance. Responses were designed to be clear, helpful, and aligned with the platform's actual capabilities.

### Transparency
The assistant communicates clearly about what it can and cannot do, preventing confusion and unrealistic expectations about automation.

### User Control
The assistant provides suggestions and guidance without removing user control over the design process. Users remain responsible for making final decisions about their yearbook layouts and content.

## Systems Thinking
The AI assistant sits within a larger product ecosystem that includes editing tools, photo management systems, and layout generation features.

Designing the assistant required understanding how users move through the entire yearbook creation workflow and identifying where conversational guidance could reduce friction.

The assistant needed to integrate with multiple parts of the platform while remaining helpful without interrupting user workflows.
