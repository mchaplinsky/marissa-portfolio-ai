# WCB Alberta Platform Redesign

## Project Overview
The WCB Alberta project focused on researching and redesigning the organization’s website and digital experience to improve navigation, accessibility, and user access to critical information. The goal was to better support employers, healthcare providers, and injured workers who relied on the website to understand policies, claims processes, and available services.

The redesign was driven by a comprehensive research phase that identified structural issues within the website’s information architecture and navigation systems.

## Industry
Insurance / Government Services

## Organization
Workers' Compensation Board of Alberta

## My Role
Senior UX Designer

## Project Scope
Conduct a full UX audit of the WCB website including accessibility testing, analytics review, competitive analysis, and extensive user research. The findings informed a redesign of the website’s information architecture and navigation systems.

The project also required developing research artifacts and strategic recommendations that would guide the organization’s long-term digital experience improvements.

## Problem
Users struggled to locate critical information on the website, particularly when trying to understand claims processes, employer responsibilities, and healthcare provider resources.

These challenges resulted in:

- high call center volume from users unable to find information  
- confusion around claims processes and documentation  
- inefficient navigation across complex content structures  
- operational strain on internal support teams  

## Users
- Employers managing workplace claims and reporting  
- Healthcare providers supporting injured workers  
- Policy teams accessing regulatory information  
- Internal WCB staff supporting claim processes  

## Research Methods
- Accessibility audit using WCAG standards  
- Google Analytics behavioral analysis  
- Competitive benchmarking against similar service organizations  
- User interviews with key audiences  
- Journey mapping across service experiences  

## Design Strategy
Reorganize the website’s structure around distinct audience groups and simplify how users navigate complex information.

The strategy focused on reducing friction in how users discover information and improving how critical processes such as claims navigation are explained and structured.

## Key UX Decisions
- Audience-based navigation structure organized by user type  
- Improved search functionality to support information discovery  
- Simplified information architecture to reduce content complexity  
- Clearer navigation pathways for key service workflows  

These improvements helped users locate important information more efficiently.

## Systems Thinking
The website functions as both a public information platform and a service gateway for multiple user groups.

The redesign required understanding how external audiences interacted with the site while also considering how internal teams relied on the platform to support claims processes and service delivery.

Mapping the broader service ecosystem helped identify structural improvements that could reduce friction across the entire user journey.

## Accessibility Considerations
- WCAG accessibility audit and recommendations  
- Improved contrast and typography readability  
- Keyboard navigation testing  
- Simplified content structure for improved comprehension  

These improvements ensured the redesigned experience remained accessible to a diverse range of users.
