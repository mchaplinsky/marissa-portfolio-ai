# SPD Criminal Record Check System

## Project Overview
The SPD Criminal Record Check Program redesign focused on modernizing the application and case management experience for the Securities Program Division in British Columbia. The goal was to replace fragmented processes with a streamlined digital platform that improved transparency for applicants while reducing operational workload for internal staff.

The redesigned system introduced structured digital workflows and role-based portals that supported both external applicants and internal regulatory teams responsible for managing applications.

## Project Scope
The project involved redesigning the entire criminal record check application system and creating a new portal for both applicants and internal staff. The redesign also consolidated more than 40 licensing types into simplified application workflows.

The system needed to support multiple user roles while maintaining regulatory compliance and operational efficiency.

## Problem
Applicants frequently contacted the organization to check the status of their applications, creating operational strain for staff. The existing system relied heavily on manual processes, fragmented workflows, and unclear application progress visibility.

Key challenges included:

- lack of transparency for applicants  
- fragmented licensing workflows  
- high volume of status inquiries from applicants  
- operational inefficiencies for staff managing applications  

These issues created delays in processing and increased administrative overhead.

## Design Strategy
The redesign focused on simplifying complex licensing workflows while improving visibility into application status for applicants and internal teams.

The platform aimed to create a more intuitive digital experience that supported both applicant submission workflows and internal case management processes.

## Key UX Decisions
- Consolidated 40+ license types into two streamlined application flows  
- Introduced role-based portals for applicants and internal staff  
- Implemented dynamic forms that adapt based on user responses  
- Added application progress tracking and status visibility  

These decisions reduced complexity for users and improved operational clarity for staff.

## Systems Thinking
The platform required coordination across multiple licensing systems, regulatory processes, and user roles. Service blueprints were used to map the full end-to-end workflow and identify opportunities to streamline interactions between applicants and internal case management teams.

This systems-level approach ensured the redesigned experience aligned both the user-facing application process and the internal operational workflows required to manage licensing requests.

## Accessibility Considerations

Accessibility considerations ensured that applicants could complete application processes regardless of digital literacy or accessibility needs.
