# CDHBC Regulatory Portal

## Project Overview
The College of Dental Hygienists of British Columbia (CDHBC) project focused on designing a modern digital platform to replace outdated paper-based administrative processes. The new system introduced administration and registrant portals that allowed staff and licensed professionals to manage licensing, documentation, and program interactions through a centralized system.

## Industry
Healthcare Regulation

## Organization
College of Dental Hygienists of British Columbia

## My Role
Senior UX Designer

## Project Scope
Design high-fidelity wireframes and interactive prototypes for administration portals, registrant portals, and internal management workflows.

## Problem
The existing system relied heavily on paper-based processes and fragmented digital tools. This made it difficult for administrators and registrants to manage licensing information, documentation, and program interactions efficiently.

## Users
- Regulatory administrators  
- Dental hygienists (registrants)  
- Organizational staff  

## Research Methods
- Stakeholder interviews  
- Usability testing sessions  
- Workflow analysis  

## Design Strategy
Create scalable administrative dashboards and workflow systems that support licensing management while maintaining strong usability for staff handling sensitive regulatory data.

## Key UX Decisions
- Centralized administrative dashboards  
- Advanced search and filtering for registrant records  
- Structured workflow interfaces  

## Systems Thinking
The portal needed to support large datasets and sensitive licensing information while enabling efficient operational workflows used by regulatory staff.

The system design required balancing usability, data organization, and permission-based access across different user roles.

## Accessibility Considerations
