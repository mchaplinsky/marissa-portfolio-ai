\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Marissa Chaplinsky Career Story\
\
Marissa Chaplinsky is a product designer who specializes in simplifying complex systems and transforming them into intuitive, scalable digital experiences. Her career has focused on designing products that sit at the intersection of user needs, operational systems, and emerging technology.\
\
Over the past eight years, Marissa has worked across industries including healthcare, government services, enterprise software, and education technology. Many of the products she has designed support large and diverse user groups such as administrators, healthcare professionals, government staff, and everyday consumers.\
\
Her work consistently centers around solving complex workflow problems. Rather than focusing only on interface design, Marissa approaches product design through systems thinking. She analyzes how users interact with platforms, how organizations deliver services through those platforms, and how design improvements can create meaningful impact for both.\
\
Early in her career, Marissa worked on large-scale public sector and enterprise platforms where usability challenges often stemmed from complicated workflows, fragmented information architecture, and operational inefficiencies. Through research, journey mapping, and service design methods, she helped organizations uncover friction points and redesign systems that improved task completion and reduced operational overhead.\
\
At Number 41 Media, she led end-to-end UX design across multiple complex platforms, conducting research audits, usability testing, and architecture redesigns that improved task success rates and reduced friction across key user journeys. Her work frequently involved CRM-driven platforms and content-heavy systems where information clarity and workflow efficiency were critical.\
\
More recently, Marissa\'92s work has expanded into designing AI-assisted product experiences. At TreeRing, she led the design of the company\'92s first AI-powered layout generation system that automatically creates yearbook page layouts based on uploaded photos. The feature significantly reduced manual page-building time while improving layout quality through structured design logic and layout constraints.\
\
She also designed an AI-powered virtual assistant that helps users navigate the platform, answer common questions, and complete tasks more efficiently. By defining guardrails, fallback states, and human handoff patterns, the system reduced reliance on manual support and improved response times across the platform.\
\
Across these projects, Marissa has developed a strong interest in how intelligent systems can support human creativity and decision-making. Her work focuses on building AI experiences that assist users through automation and suggestions while maintaining transparency and user control.\
\
Today, Marissa approaches product design as a balance between strategic thinking and practical execution. She collaborates closely with product managers, engineers, and stakeholders to translate complex requirements into usable systems that support both users and organizations.\
\
Her goal as a designer is to create products that reduce complexity, empower users, and help teams deliver meaningful services more effectively.\
