# Marissa Chaplinsky Career Story

Marissa Chaplinsky is a product designer who specializes in simplifying complex systems and transforming them into intuitive, scalable digital experiences. Her career has focused on designing products that sit at the intersection of user needs, operational systems, and emerging technology.

Over the past eight years, Marissa has worked across industries including healthcare, government services, enterprise software, and education technology. Many of the products she has designed support large and diverse user groups such as administrators, healthcare professionals, government staff, and everyday consumers.

Her work consistently centers on solving complex workflow challenges. Rather than focusing solely on interface design, Marissa approaches product design through systems thinking. She analyzes how users interact with platforms, how organizations deliver services through those platforms, and how design improvements can create meaningful impact for both users and internal teams.

---

## Early Work in Enterprise and Public Sector Systems

Early in her career, Marissa worked on large-scale public sector and enterprise platforms where usability challenges often stemmed from complex workflows, fragmented information architecture, and operational inefficiencies.

Through research, journey mapping, and service design methods, she helped organizations uncover friction points and redesign systems that improved task completion and reduced operational overhead.

At Number 41 Media, Marissa led end-to-end UX design across multiple complex platforms. Her work included research audits, usability testing, and architecture redesigns that improved task success rates and reduced friction across key user journeys.

Many of these projects involved CRM-driven platforms and content-heavy systems where information clarity and workflow efficiency were critical.

---

## Designing AI-Assisted Product Experiences

More recently, Marissa’s work has expanded into designing AI-assisted product experiences.

At TreeRing, she led the design of the company’s first AI-powered layout generation system. The feature automatically generates yearbook page layouts based on uploaded photos and image metadata. The system analyzes visual content and composition patterns to generate multiple layout suggestions that users can refine and customize.

This feature significantly reduced manual page-building time while improving layout quality through structured design logic, guardrails, and layout constraints.

She also designed an AI-powered virtual assistant that helps users navigate the platform, answer common questions, and complete tasks more efficiently.

By defining guardrails, fallback states, and human handoff patterns, the assistant reduced reliance on manual support and improved response times across the platform.

---

## Human-Centered AI Design

Across these projects, Marissa developed a strong interest in how intelligent systems can support human creativity and decision-making.

Her work focuses on designing AI experiences that assist users through automation and intelligent suggestions while maintaining transparency, reliability, and user control.

Rather than replacing human decision-making, these systems are designed to augment user capabilities and simplify complex workflows.

---

## Approach to Product Design

Today, Marissa approaches product design as a balance between strategic thinking and practical execution.

She collaborates closely with product managers, engineers, and stakeholders to translate complex requirements into usable systems that support both users and organizations.

Her goal as a designer is to create products that reduce complexity, empower users, and help teams deliver meaningful services more effectively.
