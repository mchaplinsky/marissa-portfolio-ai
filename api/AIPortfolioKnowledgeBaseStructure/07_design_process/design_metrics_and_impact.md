\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Design Metrics and Impact\
\
Design outcomes are most meaningful when they produce measurable improvements for both users and organizations. Marissa Chaplinsky evaluates design success by examining how product changes influence user behavior, operational efficiency, and overall product performance.\
\
Measuring design impact helps teams understand whether design decisions are solving the intended problems.\
\
---\
\
## Identifying Meaningful Metrics\
\
Different products require different success metrics depending on their goals and context. Marissa works with product teams to identify metrics that reflect meaningful outcomes rather than superficial engagement indicators.\
\
Common product metrics may include:\
\
\'95 task completion success rates  \
\'95 feature adoption  \
\'95 reduction in support requests  \
\'95 improvements in workflow efficiency  \
\'95 user engagement patterns\
\
Selecting the right metrics helps teams track whether design improvements are creating real value.\
\
---\
\
## Connecting Design Decisions to Business Outcomes\
\
Design decisions can influence broader organizational goals such as operational efficiency, revenue growth, and customer satisfaction.\
\
For example, improving navigation structures or simplifying workflows may reduce customer support volume or increase successful task completion.\
\
By connecting design work to these outcomes, Marissa helps teams understand the strategic value of design.\
\
---\
\
## Measuring User Experience Improvements\
\
User experience improvements can be evaluated through both qualitative and quantitative methods.\
\
Examples include:\
\
\'95 usability testing results  \
\'95 user satisfaction surveys  \
\'95 behavioral analytics\
\
These insights help teams understand how users perceive the product and where further improvements may be needed.\
\
---\
\
## Continuous Evaluation\
\
Design impact should be evaluated continuously rather than only during initial product launches.\
\
Monitoring how users interact with products over time allows teams to identify emerging challenges and opportunities for further improvement.\
